Learning Plans - week 2 and 5.

Learning Plan week 5:

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I still have so so much to learn before getting a job in tech. I will need to continue learning further with self learning online after the course, both tech and core skills. The more I learn about the challenges of code, it makes me want to eventually help people, and in particular beginners, learn to code. I think because I understand how it feels to want to develop the skills, but struggle. So possibly at some point help teach beginners, maybe high school children? Or in a community based setting. I would like anyone from any background or age or circumstances to be able to learn the skills and know they aren’t alone in struggling but also get there. There are some great resources out there, but there should be more!

I would like to be able to one day work on creating websites or apps, preferably as front-end development. I would really love to eventually work in a small collective to create websites or apps for smaller clients.

Strengths and limitations in Bootcamp?

My strengths and limitations from my week 2 are fairly similar to what I have at the moment, but my limitations are more extreme than I thought. I’m still slow at learning new material, and find it hard not to get lost in a sea of information. I find it hard to know when to stop working for a bit, and when it’s smarter to give something a break and then return to it. My strengths I thought were pushing through, but it’s also a strength to know when not to push through.

I really need to make sure I stop more before I get tired, so that I don’t spend time going backwards, or spending mine or other’s time rectifying needless mistakes I’ve made. I think it will be a challenge to talk in front of a group. It will be hard as well to ensure focus for the entirety of the 9 weeks. I actually need to make sure to eat/sleep/and rest better and make sure to do daily reflections and meditations. I’m also not good at sticking to time plans. This will be something to work at in bootcamp further.

Strengthwise I do try my hardest and don’t really give up. But interestingly…in programming I think “giving up” is not necessarily a limitation. It’s kind of connected to getting blocked. Sometimes I need to be more honest with where I’m at and look at different approaches or get the answer from google or elsewhere, or speak up when you aren’t getting anywhere.

I think I’m approachable and empathetic, which will be a strength in bootcamp.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I’d like to be more confident when talking in front of a group, or with a coding pair. I’d like to have more confidence in general. I get quite unsure of myself when I’m new to something, so often don’t speak up, or will just agree with someone else’s idea. I don’t have experience at talking in front of a group and I’d like to be able to do it, but for now the thought terrifies me. I ’d like to get better at communicating with people honestly - speaking up further when I am struggling, saying I need a break, that I still am uncertain of a concept. I would like to stop comparing myself to others.

I haven’t worked collaboratively in a creative setting with a team before, so would like to work in teams and share my work more, while also using other peoples input or code together. I'm used to doing my own creative work solo, and then presenting it to someone who says yes or not. Working with others to come up with something together will be new to me and something I'd like to feel less anxious about it. Not being afraid to show my work to others before it is finished or solved or working, and also not being afraid to suggest ideas to others, especially if I feel less technically able than them.

I still have a very long way to go when it comes to putting my reflecting skills into practice so would like to work on that more. I really want to get better at pacing myself and making sure when I need to stop and have a break, I do, and I speak up about that. I’ve gotten better at asking for help but I would like to continue this, and especially not just when I am completely blocked or in the panic-zone. I’d like to not have my stress or anxiety affect others.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I think having people to ask for help with both tech things, and core related issues. Having people understand that learning a new skill and new languages is really hard and stressful. For the bootcamp team to be patient, empathetic and kind. Being able to ask for help in person but also from behind a screen. Knowing that asking for help in itself is hard and can be a bit embarrassing. Being encouraged by the team to keep going, even when students might be frustrating with their attempts or mistakes made. Understanding some people are slower than others, and need more support than others.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I want to: speak up more when I am uncertain about something. Ask for further clarification from an instructor or student, whichever is less disruptive. In lectures, not be afraid to ask a question. I will also use the regular techniques of looking up on google and also previous foundations work. When really struggling, ask for facilitators to look at my work, maybe if they can go over material again to help me understand concepts or specific syntax better.

Workload

Every day, every day, start with reflection and meditation. And end the night in the same way. Write down an action plan for the day and week. Keep a timer so that I know when I am spending too long on a task, or too long away from a task. Know when to ask for help and do it. Know when to stop and do it. Have regular goals. When finishing a task, reflect on what I learnt before just going onto other tasks. I need to make sure every day I sleep for 7-8 hours, get some exercise, drink water, and eat properly. Communicate with people and not go into hermit mode! Speak up when struggling before it gets to be a bigger issue. When entering the panic zone or exhaustion zone just stop and don’t do anything to the computer, to the screen, to files, just stop.
